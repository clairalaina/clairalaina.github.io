Proposed Website:
Gulf Coast Gallery
By Claire Smith
Executive Summary
Gulf Coast Gallery is a website dedicated to promoting its affiliated Tampa based art
gallery. This gallery has a particular interest in supporting local Florida artists, as well
as those who come from an international background, in order to showcase Florida’s
diverse heritage. Florida is becoming a major art capital in the world, with the
influence of leading art schools nearby such as SCAD and Ringling, and growing
prestige from the international community, such as with the major art fair Art Basel
occurring every year in Miami. The Florida art market is growing, and the Gulf Coast
Gallery hopes to discover and support the artists who will be leading it. In order for its
artists to achieve significant recognition, the Gulf Coast Gallery’s website will be a
driving force for advertisement, and outreach to customers. Building a brand for each
artist, and showcasing their experiences, methods, and messages is very important.
Promoting the exhibitions, auctions, and fairs the gallery will be participating in is
another major focus of the website. Anticipation for events, and excitement will
increase attendance at the gallery, where most sales are made in person.
Goals of the Website
● To cultivate a brand for our
represented artists
● To showcase available artwork and
artist publications for consumers
● To deliver news about upcoming
events
● To showcase artwork and artists
through articles
● To engage the users with the artists
and events, to help further develop a
relationship with the gallery
● To share news related to the gallery,
artists, or the art world
The Users
The primary user of the website will
be consumers. Buyers are often
educated and wealthy individuals. A
prominent subcategory of buyers is
collectors, who collect for aesthetic
value, or financial investment as many
millennials now are. However there
are other buyers such as museums,
interior designers, and individual
consumers, who often discover
artwork through art fairs, gallery
viewing, and social media.
The Artists
The represented artists, as well as non-
represented artists will be another user of the
website. Artists can come in many forms,
however, I believe a good portion will be recent
college graduates and millennials, due to Florida's
proximity to a few renowned art schools such as
the Savannah and Ringling colleges of art and
design, as well as closeness to Disney and
Universal, which hire hundreds of beginner
artists. Older artists are also another
demographic, as Florida is known for its
retirement communities. Artists often support
other artists through purchasing their work, so
artists themselves can be target consumers.
Navigation
Designing the navigation of the site was one of the most difficult tasks. During the research process, I discovered that
many gallery sites had extensive navigation systems, and often used different verbiage for the same topics. Many galleries
also had navigation buttons for their campaigns or magazines that used site-specific jargon. I chose to use some of the
most common topics, and considering the scope of my project, felt the need to limit the number of primary and secondary
navigation options. The result is a simple, straightforward navigation system, that is not overwhelming with information.
Wireframe: The Home Page
The home page of Gulf Coast Gallery features
a scrolling news feed of recent articles and
events. Often the gallery features artist
spotlights to showcase one of its represented
artists. Exhibition announcements are also
shown here. From the home page, users can
access the main navigation bar at the top to get
to various pages on the site. To go back to the
main menu, users can click on the gallery title,
or the icon on the top left.
Wireframe: Exhibitions
These wireframes show how user interaction with
the navigation system appear. Hovering over a
primary navigation button will highlight it in aqua,
and reveal the drop down menu, and hovering over
an item in the second menu underlines it. Current
exhibitions features two previews of articles
featuring what is currently on display at the gallery.
By clicking a preview box, the user is taken to the
main article, where they can get further information.
A back button on the article side will take the user
back to the current exhibition page. The top
navigation tab, being the Exhibitions tab, is
highlighted on the navigation bar.
Wireframe: Artist Page
Each artist’s page features a variety of content. The topmost content being the
About section, and a featured art piece with a footnote holding its information.
These are shown above the fold of the page, seen in the image below. Other
features on the page include a carousel of the artist’s works, any artwork or
exhibition currently on display, with a short excerpt beside it, and articles that
showcase the artist in some way. A back button at the top leads to a page
listing the represented artists in previews. The Artist button at the top
navigation is the only navigation that doesn’t feature a second navigation menu.
Instead, users are taken straight to the Represented Artists page to view a list
of the artists.
Conclusion
The Gulf Coast Gallery strives to create a
professional website to bolster their high art
gallery through the involvement of users. The
website will be used to advertise events,
showcase artists and artwork, and to sell
pieces. A sophisticated website design with a
suitable navigation system is necessary to
convey the level of prominence the gallery
seeks in the art community. These are my
recommendations for a sleek, modern design
that will help users to navigate the website,
and explore the culture that Gulf Coast
Gallery supports.
